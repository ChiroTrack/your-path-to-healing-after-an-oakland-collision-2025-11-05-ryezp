Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about collision recovery and our chiropractic services.

It's ideal to seek chiropractic care as soon as possible after your accident, ideally within the first 24-72 hours. Even if you don't feel immediate pain, soft tissue injuries and spinal misalignments can develop over time. Early intervention helps prevent chronic complications and starts your recovery on the right track. However, it's never too late to seek care—we successfully treat patients months or even years after their accidents.

Most insurance policies, including auto insurance and health insurance, cover chiropractic treatment for collision-related injuries. We work directly with insurance providers to handle billing and documentation. Our team has extensive experience navigating insurance claims, and we'll help you understand your coverage. If you're uninsured or have questions about your specific policy, we offer flexible payment plans to ensure cost isn't a barrier to your recovery.

Your first appointment includes a comprehensive consultation, detailed physical examination, and possibly X-rays or other diagnostic imaging. We'll discuss your accident, symptoms, medical history, and recovery goals. Based on our findings, we'll explain your condition and recommend a personalized treatment plan. This initial appointment typically lasts 45-60 minutes and gives us a complete understanding of your needs so we can provide the most effective care.

Recovery time varies depending on the severity of your injuries, your overall health, and how quickly you begin treatment. Mild injuries might improve in 2-4 weeks, while more serious cases can take 8-12 weeks or longer. Some patients experience significant improvement within days, while others require ongoing care for months. We track your progress closely and adjust your treatment plan as needed. Most patients see noticeable improvements within the first 2-3 weeks of treatment.

Delayed pain is extremely common after collisions and doesn't mean your injuries are less serious. Many soft tissue injuries take hours or days to fully manifest as inflammation develops. This is why we recommend seeking care even if you feel fine immediately after the accident. If you're experiencing delayed symptoms, don't wait—contact us right away. Early treatment of delayed injuries often leads to faster recovery and better long-term outcomes.
